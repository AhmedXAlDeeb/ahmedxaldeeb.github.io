About

I'm Ahmed Soliman, a senior Biomedical Engineering student at Cairo University passionate about applying artificial intelligence to medical imaging and computer vision, particularly in brain-related applications such as fMRI analysis and visual stimulus reconstruction. My technical work spans the full pipeline — from raw neuroimaging data preprocessing to feature extraction and deep model development. Currently, my interest lies in using diffusion models and other generative techniques for brain decoding tasks.

My academic background is backed by hands-on research in brain imaging and a portfolio of projects in computer vision, signal processing, and AI. These include stress classification from ECG/PCG signals, group activity recognition, and the development of open-source educational tools to support learning in digital signal processing. I regularly work with tools like PyTorch, OpenCV, FSL, and custom neural network architectures.

Beyond technical work, I am a community-oriented person who believes in the power of mentorship and student-led impact. As Relations Manager of the IEEE EMBS Chapter at Cairo University, I lead outreach, partnership building, and technical events. I'm also proud to be a core member of DOSS (Developing Organized Students Society) — a student initiative focused on empowering peers to grow academically and personally through mentorship, workshops, and problem-solving frameworks inspired by community organizing.

My long-term goal is to pursue graduate studies and research in AI-driven medical imaging, contributing to the development of tools that improve our understanding and analysis of brain data — while continuing to support and uplift the communities around me.
