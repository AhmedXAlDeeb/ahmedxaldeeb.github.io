About Me

I’m Ahmed Soliman, a senior Biomedical Engineering student at Cairo University, graduating in 2026. My journey bridges research and industry, focusing on leveraging artificial intelligence for medical imaging, neuroimaging, anomaly detection, human-computer interaction, and digital signal processing. I am particularly interested in understanding and interpreting brain function, with projects spanning fMRI analysis, visual stimulus reconstruction, and AI-generated content detection.

Technically, I work across the full AI pipeline — from data acquisition and preprocessing to deep learning model design, evaluation, and deployment. I have applied these skills in both academic research, such as early detection of papilledema from fundus images, VR-based rehabilitation studies, and multimodal stress detection, as well as industry projects including credit scoring, customer analytics, and building a Multi-Agent System for financial growth and investment coaching.

I frequently work with PyTorch, TensorFlow, OpenCV, and FSL for research, and SQL, Docker, and REST APIs for production-grade pipelines. My experience reflects a synergy between scientific exploration and applied engineering, developing solutions that are both innovative and deployable.

Beyond technical work, I am committed to leadership and outreach. As President of DOSS CUFE and Relations Manager of IEEE EMBS Cairo University, I have led initiatives that connect students with academia and industry, fostering mentorship and collaborative problem-solving.

My long-term ambition is to pursue graduate research in applied AI and medical imaging, creating tools that bridge engineering and healthcare to enhance human well-being.
